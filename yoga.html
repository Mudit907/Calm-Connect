Importance of Yoga

  • Enhances flexibility and posture
  • Reduces stress and anxiety naturally
  • Improves breathing and energy levels
  • Boosts concentration and mindfulness
  • Promotes overall physical and mental well-being

Yoga Asanas

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Stand tall with your feet together, arms by your sides, and palms facing forward. Lengthen your spine, engage your core, and lift your chest. This pose improves posture and blood circulation, which nourishes the skin and promotes a healthy glow.

Sarvangasana (Shoulder Stand)

According to Himalayan Siddhaa Akshar, Founder of Akshar Yoga Kendraa, “Lie on your back and lift your legs and lower body off the ground, supporting your lower back with your hands. Keep your legs straight and your toes pointed upward. Shoulder stand increases blood flow to the face, reducing wrinkles and promoting a youthful complexion.”

Halasana (Plow Pose)

From shoulder stand, lower your legs over your head until your feet touch the ground behind you. Keep your hands on your lower back for support. This pose stimulates the thyroid gland, which regulates metabolism and can improve skin texture.

Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

According to Yogacharya Akhil Gore, Founder RouteIn Yoga, “Lie on your stomach with your palms flat on the ground under your shoulders. Inhale as you lift your chest off the ground, keeping your elbows close to your body. Cobra pose stretches the chest and promotes circulation, which rejuvenates the skin.”

Matsyasana (Fish Pose)

Lie on your back with your legs extended and your arms resting by your sides. Press your forearms and elbows into the ground as you lift your chest toward the ceiling. Fish pose opens the throat and chest, allowing for deeper breathing and increased oxygen flow to the skin.

Watch Video

Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)

Sit on the floor with your legs extended in front of you. Hinge at the hips and reach forward, grabbing hold of your feet or shins. This pose calms the mind and reduces stress, which can minimize breakouts and promote clearer skin.

Watch Video

Viparita Karani (Legs-Up-The-Wall Pose)

Sit close to a wall and lie on your back, extending your legs upward along the wall. Relax your arms by your sides and close your eyes. Legs-up-the-wall pose promotes relaxation and improves blood circulation, reducing puffiness and dark circles around the eyes.
